PEP Competition

In July of 2021, MSS will compete in the PEP for small craft racing competition. It is a 5-mile race with our electric boat in Baltimore, MD. This competition gives students an opportunity to work with design software, a variety of tools, and collaborate to create an innovative final product. MSS and our competitors are tasked with completing 5 laps in a short out and back circuit in the waters of Curtis Creek and Arundel Cove. The top three winners will be awarded $3,000, $2,000, and $1,000 at the conclusion of the competition. Participants in the competition will be tasked with completing 5 laps of a short (less than 1 mile) out and back circuit in relatively protected waters of Curtis Creek and Arundel Cove. The competition is open to both manned and unmanned vessels, but participation is subject to compliance with all USCG regulations. Manned and unmanned vessels will race separately for safety reasons. Additionally, the competition organizers reserve the right to decline any vessel water access if safety concerns are present.

Our Preparation

Research and Design

Research and Design are critical for growth. Without having a boat from previous years to evolve, we have the opportunity to choose a technological path. Investigating and developing technologies is key in our performance.

Data Collection

Data Collection allows us to learn from and optimize our work. We use sensor data to track what our boat is seeing and how it is reacting. This information can then be used to further develop our software and train our pilot.

Calculations

Calculations are the backbone of our technical progression and performance. It’s important that we can implement what we learn in class to quantify designs and program our boat to make the right moves.

Team Leadership Meetings

Team Leadership Meetings ensure the subteams are working as a cohesive unit and give all team members the opportunity to contribute to big decisions. These open meetings also foster a multidisciplinary progress.

Weekly Subteam Meetings

Weekly Subteam Meetings keep our team members on the same page and help us plan our activities. At these meetings, project leads present on their work so that our members drive developmental ideas and learn from each other.

Team Member Training

Team Member Training is integral to ensuring that our members have the tools and knowledge necessary to succeed. We train our members in a variety of software, machinery, and other programs to enhibit this success.

Underclassmen Recruiting

Underclassmen Recruiting gives younger students opportunities to take on leadership roles and apply their theoretical knowledge to real-world situations. Students can explore diverse interests and find areas of specialization.

Networking

Networking with sponsors, peers, and faculty is essential to the development MSS. We seek to foster new connections our members can utilize to grow their knowledge base and develop relationships.

Sponsorship Outreach

Sponsorship Outreach ensures that our team has the funds, knowledge, and support necessary to innovate throughout the year. Maintaining these relationships is vital to the continued success of our project team in the future.
